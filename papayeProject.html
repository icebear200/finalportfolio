Papaye Restaurant Redesign

A Hidden Gem: Papaye Restaurant in New York City

One of my treasured spots in New York City is Papaye Restaurant, a local family-run gem in the Bronx serving delicious Ghanaian and African dishes that resonate with my upbringing in Ghana. For this project, I took on the task of revamping Papaye's outdated site and ensuring a smoother user experience while staying true to the authentic flavors that Papaye embodies.

THE PROBLEM

The original Papaye site, created in 2006, has seen little updating since then. The homepage, dark and outdated, suffers from corrupted image files that fail to load, leaving it mostly empty with just a black background. Despite having a food gallery with updating images of the dishes the restaurant offers, the images don't load, creating a lack of visual appeal that fails to showcase Papaye's delicious offerings. Because of this, the site lacks a clear color theme and brand identity. This low contrast and lack of imagery might lead users to believe the site is out-of-date and exit the site prematurely before viewing information about the restaurant's food offerings and locations.

Why it Matters:

Choosing to address and update the issues on Papaye's website was important because it provided a free design solution to a small business run by local African immigrants for whom this restaurant is the achievement of their American dream. Further, the improvements I made to the site will help attract and retain customers, improve accessibility, and help Papaye create a stronger and more recognizable brand identity.

RESEARCH

Research that Influenced my Work:

I conducted a comparative analysis of other restaurant sites to see what worked best. I liked sites with simple designs because they had a clear user flow. I also noticed a lot of sites utilized flat designs for buttons and links which helped create a more modern feel that I wanted to bring to Papaye's site. Additionally, all these sites had a strong sense of branding either through color palettes, fonts, or themes. This influenced me to be thoughtful and look for fonts and color palettes that would complement the diverse and colorful foods I wanted to display on Papaye's site through the food gallery.

The audience of this site is mainly New York City locals who are familiar restaurant. With little to no advertising, the restaurant relies on proximity and word of mouth for recognition. Primarily, patrons are Ghanaian and other African immigrants seeking familiar cuisine. However, the restaurant also attracts a diverse crowd due to its location in a predominantly immigrant area.

PROCESS

All in all, my final design didn't differ too much from the sketches and high-fidelity prototype because I spent a lot of time on the sketching process.

Step 1: Wireframe sketch of the site including desired functionality. Navigation bar to improve user flow through the site, an updating gallery updating images of Papaye's offerings.

Step 2: Created a hi-fidelity prototype including clear branding through, color palette, food images, and fonts.

Final Design: In the end, I changed the color palette for higher contrast colors after discovering issues with accessibility scanners. I also redesigned the fonts to be more artistic and playful, adding personality to the site and brand. Additionally, I incorporated "View on Map" buttons, which my previous designs lacked. Including this allows users to to easily navigate from their location to the restaurant, improving my overall user flow.

SOLUTION

To address these issues, I redesigned the website into a single-page format that streamlines all the information. At the top, I added a navigation bar with buttons for easy scrolling to the "About" and "Hours and Location" sections. I also updated the "Order Online" button to directly link to the restaurant's Doordash ordering page. For a cohesive look, I used a white background, consistent color palette, and font theme throughout the site. The centerpiece of the redesign is a food gallery that showcases the restaurant's offerings with updating images, creating a visually appealing and informative experience for users.

Using the Navigation Bar to Improve User Flow

I added a navigator bar with buttons that allow users to easily navigate the page. For example, clicking the "Hours and Locations" button scrolls the page to where this information is available.

In addition to scrolling to different sections, buttons on the page guide users to useful information like directions to the restaurant and the online ordering platform. This enhances user experience by providing easy access to important details.

Addressing Lack of Photos, Contrast, and Brand Identity: Redesigned Food Gallery

To maintain the original design's vision of showcasing the restaurant's cuisine, the homepage now includes a dynamic food gallery. This gallery continuously updates with images of the delicious dishes, enticing visitors and reflecting the restaurant's current offerings.

Responsive Design and Accessibility

The website now seamlessly adapts to various devices-- desktops, smartphones, or tablets. This ensures that users can enjoy the same experience, regardless of their device.

TAKEAWAYS

This redesign taught me to be adaptable. While my final design didn't stray too far from my original vision, I had to make some sacrifices to prioritize accessibility. I changed my color palette, font style, and some images to ensure better contrast and readability for users. This process also highlighted the importance of branding, as I aimed to balance modernity with the restaurant's authenticity.
